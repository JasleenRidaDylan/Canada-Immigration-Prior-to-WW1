CANADA IMMIGRATION PRIOR TO WW1

Due to the recently completed Railway connecting the country together which made it possible for people and goods to moved to the central part of the country which used to much more difficult. It made it economically feasible to sent new people out to the west to build up a economy over there increasing the wealth of Canada.

Canada's population which in 1902 was 5 million increased to 8 million by 1914. Most of these immigrants were drawn to the prairies due to the promise of 160 acres of free land. This period of mass immigration was know as the Third Wave.
